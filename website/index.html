Why grammar matters

Languages are patterns before they are memorized lists.

Vocabulary matters, but grammar gives you the structure to understand, interpret, and create far more than a phrasebook can cover.

Understanding grammar speeds up learning because you can recognize recurring sentence shapes faster.
Example sentences reinforce patterns by showing how grammar changes meaning in context.
When learners understand structure, they become less dependent on memorizing entire sentences one by one.
Platform principle

Keystone teaches the system underneath the sentence.

Instead of chasing random examples, the platform helps learners notice the repeated grammar patterns that make those examples understandable.
